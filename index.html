What you need to know

99.9% Uptime

Business email should never be down.

While email servers crashing and getting restored within a few hours without losing data isnt a big deal as email servers will re-send emails for 48 hours, email is a critical communication tool. You also need it to sign up for stuff and recover forgotten passwords.
It's a huge pain and email failure should just not be tolerated today.
What you need is a vendor that runs email in a highly available infrastructure that just works.

5GB per user

Nobody deletes email anymore

Search in emails has gotten so powerful that people dont really have to organize their email anymore. Especially for a business where all correspondence should be kept just in case. You'll want enough disk space to last years of use.

IMAP + Pop3 Access

Access your email from your current email client

Nobody wants to have to get used to new software just for email. Most people have multiple email accounts set up and it's outrageous to have to force any of your colleagues to install new software to use their business email.

Web Mail

An easy to use, fast loading web interface

There will always be times when you need to access your email on a public or borrowed computer where a web interface to check your company email would come in handy.
Some light email users even make do without an installed mail client and use web mail exclusively.

What are the options?

Besides Zoho Mail, there are no other free email services that host your custom domain. Both Google Apps and Microsoft 365 have stopped their official free programs and closed up loopholes that used to be available.

  • Zoho Workplace

    Free for 25 users

  • Google Apps for Work

    $50/user/year

  • Microsoft Office 365 Business Essentials

    $60/user/year

  • Atmail

    $24/user/year

If you want email for free, just sign up for Zoho Workplace. Their user interface isnt as smooth as the others and behind Google & Microsoft in spam filtering.

Considering a paid service? There are a few things to consider, like if you need a PC installed Microsoft Word Documents or are happy with a browser based solution like Google Docs. File sharing and other collaborative working needs also play a large part in your decision.
